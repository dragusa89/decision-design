Workshop

Four hours.
Eight to twelve people.
One framework.

A small-room workshop for senior freelancers who are tired of competing on deliverables. We do the framework together, and by the end of the afternoon you'll see the decision moments in your own practice that you've been missing.

№ 10 — Workshop Length 4 hrs Seats 8–12
What It Is

A small room.
The framework.
Your own cases.

The workshop is the first time most attendees see Decision Design in full. We move through the framework piece by piece — but the cases on the table are yours. By the second hour, the room has stopped talking about my examples and started talking about its own.

The format is deliberately small. Twelve seats, no more. Everyone speaks. Nothing is recorded.

The workshop also qualifies. Packages and retainers are offered only after, and only to those who are clearly ready. The room itself is the diagnostic.

The Afternoon

Four parts.
Each one is the room talking.

i.

The frame

Why decisions don't happen in the meeting. The vocabulary of the practice — frame, leverage, uncertainty, authority. Spoken in plain language with examples.

14:00 — 14:5050 minutes
ii.

Your moment before the moment

Each participant brings one situation. We map them on the table — what frame was set, who set it, what the decision actually was. The room sees the pattern fast.

15:00 — 16:2080 minutes
iii.

The next conversation

We rehearse. Each person identifies one upcoming conversation — a pricing call, a pitch, a scope negotiation — and works through how to set the frame this time. Out loud, in the room.

16:30 — 17:3060 minutes
iv.

Quiet close

A short written exercise. Each person leaves with their own one-page frame. No certificates. No applause. The work is the reward.

17:30 — 18:0030 minutes

Who It's For

  • Senior freelancers (5+ yrs).
  • Independent designers.
  • Studio principals of one or two.
  • Anyone tired of explaining their price.

If the framework is new to you and your work isn't yet senior, the Diagnostic is a better starting point.

Logistics

  • Budapest, in person. Weekday afternoons.
  • Eight to twelve seats per cohort.
  • Held three to four times per year.
  • EN or HU — confirmed per cohort.

Lunch is served before; coffee runs throughout. Materials kept by you.
